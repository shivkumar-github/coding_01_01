Food for Animals

Why donate food for animals?

Donating food for animals is an expression of stewardship, utilizing the resources bestowed upon us to provide for those who lack the means to vocalize their needs. It underscores our commitment to compassionate care, ensuring the well-being of creatures reliant on our benevolence. In this manner, we honor our duty to uphold the sanctity of life and fulfill our role as custodians of the environment.

Which animals do we serve?

  • Cows and buffaloes: They are our prime focus. We feed them fresh-green grass, jaggery, special wheat-rotis, vegetables and seasonal fruits like watermelons.
  • Camels: We feed camels with grass and food similar to what we give to cows.
  • Goats: We feed goats with fresh-green grass.
  • Rabbits: We feed rabbits with vegetables such as carrots. (Rabbits, not much in numbers, are fed occasionaly and based on donor's requests.)
  • Ants: We feed ants with special food made for them by grinding sugar and cocunut.
  • Crows: We feed crows with gaathiya(a chickpea-flour based food item).

How can you donate?

There are two ways in which you can donate:

  • Donate the food items for the animals
  • Donate the money for food items

If you wish to donate the food items for animals, you must fill-out the below form for food items and we will get in touch with you. If you wish to donate money for the same, you can transfer it online(via UPI, and then fill the below form) or hand it over to your nearest volunteer. If the amount is large, we may collect it from your location.
